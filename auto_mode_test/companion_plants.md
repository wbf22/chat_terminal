# Companion Planting Chart

This chart lists common garden vegetables, herbs, and flowers with good and bad companion plants and the primary benefits of pairing them. Use this as a quick reference when planning beds or containers.

| Plant | Good Companions | Bad Companions | Benefits / Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tomato | Basil, Marigold, Chives, Carrots, Lettuce | Corn (competes for space), Potatoes (disease risk) | Basil may improve flavor and repel pests; marigolds deter nematodes; chives reduce fungal disease. |
| Basil | Tomato, Peppers, Oregano | Rue | Repels flies and mosquitoes; improves growth/ flavor of tomatoes. |
| Pepper | Basil, Onions, Spinach, Carrots | Beans (may stunt), Fennel | Basil repels pests; onions reduce aphids. |
| Carrot | Onion, Leek, Rosemary, Sage, Peas | Dill (when seed-setting), Fennel | Onions deter carrot fly; herbs repel pests. |
| Onion / Garlic | Carrots, Beets, Lettuce, Cabbage family | Beans, Peas (garlic may inhibit legumes) | Strong scents repel pests (aphids, carrot fly). |
| Beans (Bush / Pole) | Corn, Cucumber, Radish, Carrots | Onions, Garlic, Fennel | Fix nitrogen for heavy-feeding neighbors; corn provides trellis for pole beans. |
| Peas | Carrots, Radishes, Turnips, Beans | Onions, Garlic | Nitrogen-fixers improve soil for leafy crops. |
| Corn | Beans, Squash (Three Sisters method) | Tomatoes (shade/competition) | Beans fix nitrogen; squash shades soil to reduce weeds/retain moisture. |
| Cucumber | Beans, Radish, Dill, Nasturtium | Aromatic herbs like Sage (can reduce growth) | Nasturtiums deter beetles; radishes can trap pests. |
| Squash / Pumpkin | Corn, Beans, Nasturtium, Marigold | Potatoes | Nasturtiums and marigolds help deter squash beetles; corn and beans form Three Sisters. |
| Lettuce | Carrots, Radishes, Strawberries, Cucumbers | Cabbage family (can overshadow), Fennel | Fast-growing; works well as a filler under tall plants for shade. |
| Spinach | Strawberries, Peas, Radish | Potatoes | Beneficial as a cool-season understory; peas add nitrogen. |
| Cabbage family (Broccoli, Kale, Cabbage) | Dill, Onions, Garlic, Thyme, Nasturtium | Strawberries (pests), Tomatoes (different soil needs) | Strong-scented herbs and nasturtiums deter caterpillars and cabbage moths. |
| Marigold | Tomatoes, Squash, Cucumbers, Beans, Almost everything | None major — may inhibit some legumes in high density | Deters nematodes and many pests; good general companion plant. |
| Nasturtium | Squash, Cucumber, Tomato, Cabbage family | Beans (can compete) | Attracts aphids away from vegetables (trap crop); deters squash bugs. |
| Rosemary, Thyme, Sage | Cabbage family, Beans, Carrots, Drought-tolerant borders | Heavy feeders (avoid planting next to high-water veg) | Aromatic herbs repel many pests; attract pollinators. |
| Dill | Cabbage family, Cucumbers | Carrots (when too close can stunt) | Attracts beneficial predatory insects (parasitic wasps); good near brassicas. |

Notes and tips:
- Companion planting is not a guarantee of pest control or increased yield but can improve biodiversity, attract beneficial insects, reduce pest pressure, and make efficient use of space.
- Some combinations depend on spacing and timing (e.g., onions and beans can be fine if not crowded; fennel is generally best kept separate).
- Rotate crops yearly to reduce disease and nutrient depletion.

Sources & further reading:
- Local extension services and regional planting guides are recommended since companion effects can vary by climate and soil.
